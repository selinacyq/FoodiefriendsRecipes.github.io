Vegan Mapo Tofu

Vegan Mapo Tofu is a plant-based variation of the traditional Sichuan dish. It emerged as a vegan-friendly alternative to accommodate dietary preferences and restrictions. This version typically replaces the minced meat with ingredients like mushrooms or textured vegetable protein while retaining the signature spicy and savory flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 4 or 5 dried shiitake mushroom
  • Moderate amount of salt
  • 14 to 16 ounces (390 to 450 grams) of firm tofu, cut into 3/4-inch cubes
  • 1 tablespoon of potato starch
  • 3 teaspoons of whole peppercorns
  • 5 dried red chiles, cut into 3/4-inch segments and seeds shaken out
  • 1 tablespoon of peeled and miced fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon of minced fresh garlic
  • 1 tablespoon of fermented black beans, coarsely chopped
  • 2½ tablespoons of chili bean paste
  • 1 teaspoon of chili flakes
  • 1 cup of water
  • ½ teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of soy sauce
  • 2 scallions, green parts only, thinly sliced, for garnish
  • ½ teaspoon of ground peppercorns

Instructions:

  1. Rehydrate the mushrooms with soak it in hot water for 30 minutes, then drain, stem and finely chop them.

  2. Meanwhile, boil a pot of salted water. Reduce the heat to low, add the tofu cubes, and simmer gently for 10 minutes to refresh the tofu's flavors and firm it up.

  3. Make a slurry by combining the starch with ¼ cup cold water in a small bowl. Stir until smooth and set aside.

  4. Heat a wok over medium heat until a bead of water evaporates immediately upon contact. Add the oil, swirling to the coat sides of the wok. Reduce the heat to low. Add the whole Sichuan peppercorns and dried chiles and stir-fry for 1 to 2 minutes to infuse the oil with flavor, until the chiles are slightly darkened in color and aromatic. Do not burn them. Remove from the heat. Using a slotted spoon, remove and discard the spices, leaving behind the aromatic oil.

  5. Return the wok to medium-high heat and add the mushrooms, ginger, and garlic. Fry for 1 minute, until the mushrooms are beginning to brown. Scoot them up one side of the wok.

  6. Add the fermented black beans, chili bean paste, and ground chile and stir-fry briefly for 10 seconds to release their fragrance. Pour in the stock, then add sugar and soy sauce. Bring the liquid to a boil. Lift the tofu cubes from the hot water with a slotted spoon and place them gently in the wok. To keep them from breaking, don’t stir; instead, move the wok in a swirling motion, shifting the sauce as it bubbles under the tofu. Bring to a full boil.

  7. Give the starch slurry a stir and drizzle about a third of it into the wok, swirling the wok gently to mix in the slurry as the liquid thickens. Repeat this two more times, until the sauce is glossy and clings to the tofu, then remove the wok from the heat and transfer everything to a serving dish. Sprinkle with the scallions and the ground peppercorns (these provide the tingly mouthfeel) and serve.

Stir-Fried Egg and Tomato

Juicy tomatoes mingle with softly scrambled eggs in a saucy dish that's delicious over rice. There's a reason this quick, satisfying meal is a staple of home cooking.

Ingredients:

  • 6 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil, divided
  • 2 scallions, finely chopped (reserve some chopped greens for garnish)
  • 4 medium tomatoes (about 1 pound), each cut into 6 wedges
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of salt

Instructions:

  1. Beat eggs with ½teaspoon salt until smooth but not frothy.

  2. Heat 1 tablespoon oil over medium-high heat until hot. Add eggs and cook until a thin film of cooked egg forms on bottom of skillet but most of eggs are still runny, 5 to 10 seconds. Immediately pick up eggs into a bowl.

  3. Heat remaining tablespoon oil over medium-high heat until hot. Add scallions and stir-fry until just softened, about 30 seconds.

  4. Add tomatoes and cook, until juices are released, 4 to 6 minutes. Sprinkle sugar and ¼ teaspoon salt over tomatoes and stir to combine.

  5. Return eggs to cook, stirring occasionally, until eggs are just cooked through.

  6. Serve sprinkled with reserved scallion greens.

Lucky 8 Stir-Fry

In Chinese culture, the number eight is considered lucky, symbolizing prosperity. This mixed vegetable dish draws inspiration from Buddhist vegetarian cooking and incorporates ingredients representing good luck, prosperity, happiness, family, and longevity. With contrasting flavors and textures, it can be served any day, especially during Lunar New Year.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 1 cup of bean sprouts
  • 3 inner stalks celery hearts, cut on the bias ¼ inch thick
  • 4 to 6 medium dried shiitake mushrooms, soaked in warm water for 2 to 3 hours
  • 1 medium carrot, cut into ¼-inch-thick strips
  • ½ cup of dried lily flowers, soaked in warm water for 30 minutes
  • ½ cup of dried wood ear mushrooms, soaked in warm water for 30 minutes and cut into ¼-inch-thick strips
  • 1 cup of sliced Chinese cabbage or baby bok choy
  • 8 snow peas, trimmed and cut on the bias into ½-inch-wide pieces
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of water
  • 1 teaspoon Shaoxing wine, sherry, or dry Marsala wine
  • ¼ teaspoon of sesame oil
  • ⅛ teaspoon of white pepper powder
  • ¼ teaspoon pf salt (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat a wok over high heat until wisps of smoke rise from the surface. Swirl in the vegetable oil and heat for a few seconds until it starts to shimmer.

  2. Add all of the vegetables: bean sprouts, celery, shiitake mushrooms, carrot, dried lily flowers, wood ear mushrooms, Chinese cabbage or baby bok choy, and snow peas. Stir-fry for about 90 seconds.

  3. Then add the soy sauce, water, and Shaoxing wine. Stir-fry for about 1 minute.

  4. Add the sesame oil and white pepper powder. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds more to combine.

  5. Turn off the heat. Taste for seasoning. If you think it needs a pinch of salt, add the salt and stir to combine. Transfer to a serving dish.

Braised Winter Squash With Fermented Black Beans

In this dish, the squash’s natural sweetness is complemented by the salty, savory fermented black beans, and the squash is cooked until buttery and tender, on the verge of falling apart.It is a rare delicacy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound (450 grams) of winter squash
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 4 or 5 dried red chiles, snipped into ½-inch pieces and seeds shaken out
  • 1 tablespoon of fermented black beans, coarsely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup of unsalted stock of any kind or water
  • 1 teasppon of sugar
  • ½ teasppon of salt
  • ½ teasppon of toasted sesame oil
  • Ground white pepper
  • 1 scallion, green part only, thinly sliced, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Peel and core the squash and cut it into 1½-inch wedges, then cut the wedges into pieces ½ inch thick and 1½ inches wide; you should have about 3 cups.

  2. Heat a wok over high heat and add the scallion oil, swirling to coat the sides. Add the dried chiles and stir-fry until they begin to darken in color, about 10 seconds.

  3. Add the fermented black beans and garlic and let them sizzle until fragrant, about 30 seconds.

  4. Add the squash pieces and stir them around the wok to pick up all the fragrant oil, then pour in the stock and bring to a boil.

  5. Season with the sugar and salt, then cover, reduce the heat to maintain a gentle simmer, and cook until the squash has absorbed most of the liquid and is tender enough to pierce with a chopstick, 4 to 5 minutes.

  6. Uncover and taste for salt and sugar; adjust the seasoning if necessary. Remove from the heat, stir in the sesame oil and a dash of white pepper, scatter the sliced scallions on top, and serve.
