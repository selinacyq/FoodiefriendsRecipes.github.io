Nasi Lemak

Nasi Lemak is a Malaysian national dish that consists of fragrant rice cooked in coconut milk and pandan leaves, served with various side dishes such as spicy sambal, fried anchovies, peanuts, cucumber and boiled or fried egg. It is a popular breakfast dish in Malaysia.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of rice
  • 3 cups of water
  • 1 cup of coconut milk
  • 1 pandan leaf
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 cucumbers, sliced
  • 4 small chili peppers, sliced
  • 4 tablespoons of fried anchovies
  • 4 tablespoons of peanuts, roasted
  • Sambal (chili paste)

Instructions:

  1. Wash the rice and drain.

  2. Combine the rice, water, coconut milk, pandan leaf, salt, and sugar in a rice cooker and cook.

  3. While the rice is cooking, fry the eggs sunny-side-up.

  4. Once the rice is done, serve it on a plate with the fried eggs, sliced cucumbers, sliced chili peppers, fried anchovies, and roasted peanuts on top.

  5. Serve with sambal on the side.

Roti Canai

Roti Canai is a type of Indian-influenced flatbread that is usually served with curry or dhal. It is made by kneading dough with ghee (clarified butter), water, flour and a pinch of salt, and then cooking it on a hot griddle. Roti Canai is a popular breakfast item in Malaysia and is often served with a cup of teh tarik (pulled tea).

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 1/2 cup of condensed milk
  • 1 egg
  • Vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add water, condensed milk, and egg, and mix well to form a smooth dough.

  2. Divide the dough into small balls. Roll out each ball thinly on a floured surface.

  3. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat. Add a little vegetable oil and fry the roti canai until golden brown on both sides.

  4. Serve hot with dhal curry or chicken curry.

Kaya Toast

Kaya Toast is a traditional breakfast dish in Malaysia that consists of toasted bread spread with Kaya, a sweet coconut jam made with eggs, sugar and coconut milk. The bread is usually served with a side of soft-boiled eggs, and is often accompanied by a cup of coffee or tea.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of white bread
  • 2 tablespoons of kaya jam
  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • 2 soft boiled eggs
  • Soya sauce and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Toast the bread until it is crispy and golden brown.

  2. Spread the kaya jam and butter on one slice of the toast.

  3. Break the soft boiled eggs into a bowl and add a dash of soya sauce and pepper.

  4. Dip the other slice of toast into the egg mixture and enjoy.

Big Breakfast

Big Breakfast is a Western-inspired breakfast dish that is popular in Malaysia. It typically consists of a combination of bacon, sausages, eggs, baked beans, mushrooms, grilled tomatoes and toast. Some variations may include hash browns or black pudding. Big Breakfast is usually served with coffee or tea.

Ingredients

  • 2 Pork Sausages
  • 2 rashers lean back bacon,
  • Button mushrooms
  • 1 small onion, cut into rings
  • 1 tomato, halved
  • 1 medium boiled potato, cubed
  • 1/2 cup baked beans
  • 1 or 2 large eggs

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-hot. Grill the sausages for 5 minutes, turning frequently. Add the bacon to the wire rack, grill for 5 minutes on each side or until the sausages are cooked through.

  2. Meanwhile, spray a non-stick pan with low-calorie cooking spray and add the button mushrooms, onion, tomato and potato. Fry until the mushrooms, tomato and onions are softened and the potato is golden brown.

  3. Heat the beans in a small pan until piping hot.

  4. Crack the eggs into a non-stick pan and cook to your liking. Assemble your breakfast on a large plate, adding brown sauce or tomato ketchup
